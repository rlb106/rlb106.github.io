National Library of Medicine

The Exhibition Program at the National Institute of Health's Library of Medicine creates lively and informative exhibitions and educational resources that enhance awareness of and appreciation for the collections. While working at Riggs Ward Design, I designed several rotating banner exhibitions in two formats: a semi-permanent installation for their library gallery space, and a mobile traveling version to be rented out to Libraries across the country.

Virginia Tech Visitor Center

While working at Riggs Ward Design, I worked collaboratively with a team of architects to produce informative signage, banners, and the layout of a complex media-rich timeline wall. These were produced to be visually consistent with and in support of the university's existing visual brand standards.

Flowerdew Hundred: Unearthing Virginia's History

The exhibit is on permanent display at the Mary and David Harrison Institute for American History, Literature, and Culture at the University of Virginia. Featuring archaeological artifacts from the University of Virginia Library’s Flowerdew Hundred Collection, the permanent exhibit provides a unique look at some of Virginia's earliest inhabitants. While at Riggs Ward Design, I worked collaboratively with an architect to produce all of the graphic panels to be installed in the final exhibit.

The Doyle Program for Engaging Difference

The Doyle Program serves the Georgetown University community is designed to deepen the universities own commitment to tolerance and diversity and enhance global awareness of the challenges and opportunities of an era of increasing interconnectedness. The Doyle Annual Report publication falls under the responsibilities of the designer working in CNDLS, but previously had no consistent visual branding developed and applied to the program. Based on the few previous consistent elements the program had used in the form of a tree motif, I developed a visual system that has since been applied to the programs publications, event materials, digital marketing efforts, and program web presence.

To view the live version of the website, please visit doyle.georgetown.edu.

Future(s) of the University

Georgetown launched “Designing The Future(s) of the University” as an integrative initiative to engage the university community in an exploration of issues facing higher education and to actively experiment with new ways to deliver Georgetown’s signature education. Working collaboratively with several offices across campus, I helped to develop a visual style for the program that was immediately translated into a Wordpress blog site to house the initiative's efforts.

To view the live version of the website, please visit futures.georgetown.edu.

Genomic Medicine Gets Personal

The GeorgetownX team worked with a large group of faculty and medical professionals in the development of a massive open online course (MOOC) for the edX platform. During the development of this course, I helped to create a set of visual standards and templated graphics to be applied across all video content while working closely with the medical faculty to illustrate complex medical information in a format to be understood by the average student not of a medical background.

To view the whole contents of this course, please register for the course at edx.org.
